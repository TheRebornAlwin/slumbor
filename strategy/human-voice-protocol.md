# The Human Voice Protocol (Slumbor copy bible)

Research-backed rules for writing copy that reads like a real person, not AI.
Applies to all Slumbor first-person / brand-voice content.

## Two registers (READ THIS FIRST)

There are two ways to apply this, depending on where the copy lives:

1. **Page copy** (headings, body, section prose, buy box, FAQ): keep **perfect
   grammar** — full sentences, proper capitalization, real commas. The "human"
   part means: vary sentence length hard (mix short punchy lines with long ones),
   cut every AI tell below, allow a little honest edge/frustration, stay concrete.
   It should read like a sharp, real person wrote it, not like a marketing email.
   No lowercase chaos, no fragments-as-style here.

2. **Testimonials / reviews / ad scripts / social captions**: go fully casual —
   lowercase, fillers, fragments, run-ons, self-interruptions, lived-experience
   asides, anti-sales energy. The full Fifteen Commandments below apply.

## Core rule
You are not writing, you are transcribing what a specific real person said. If a
line could appear in a marketing email or an AI assistant reply, it failed. AI
defaults to low perplexity + low burstiness (predictable words, uniform 15-22 word
sentences, clean SVO grammar, balanced positive tone). Work against that.

## The Fifteen Commandments (full casual register)
1. **Vary sentence length hard.** 1-word fragments next to 40-word run-ons. In casual
   copy: ≥30% of sentences ≤8 words, ≥15% ≤4 words, ≥1 sentence >25 words per para.
2. **Fillers required:** like, you know, I mean, okay so, honestly, literally, actually,
   kind of, basically, I guess, or whatever, right?, well, anyway. ~1 per 30-50 words
   (sprinkled, not piled).
3. **Self-interruption / false starts.** "I went to— actually no, that was Tuesday."
4. **Random lived-experience details** that don't sell the product (the dog stared at
   me, it was raining, my AirPods were dead). Strongest signal of human authorship.
5. **Contradictions and hedges.** "it's amazing. well, for me. maybe not for everyone."
6. **Negativity / frustration**, even in positive copy. AI has positivity bias.
7. **Lowercase + loose punctuation** for written casual voice; ALL CAPS for emphasis.
8. **Topic jumps, no transitions.**
9. **Weird word choices (perplexity spikes):** "destroyed" not "very tired", "I was
   just gone" not "I fell asleep", "my whole face unclenched."
10. **Specific numbers OR vague time**, never the AI middle ("about three weeks").
11. **Reference people/things without explaining them** (Becca, the Goop thing).
12. **Anti-sales energy.** "I'm not gonna tell you this changed my life." Reluctance =
    human, sales energy = AI.

## Banned vocabulary (NEVER use, page or casual)
delve, leverage, unlock, elevate, robust, comprehensive, tapestry, realm, navigate
(metaphorical), embark, foster, harness, transformative, empower, streamline,
holistic, seamless, vibrant, pivotal, meticulous, significantly, plethora, in
conclusion, furthermore, moreover, additionally, in today's world, it's important to
note, when it comes to, a wide array of, "the perfect blend of", "stands as a
testament to", "the intersection of".

## Banned structures (NEVER use)
- Three-part symmetric phrasing / tricolons: "calming, soothing, and restorative."
- "Not just X, but Y": "It's not just a mask, it's a ritual."
- "Whether you're X or Y..." openers.
- "Imagine [hypothetical]..." openers.
- "Are you tired of...?" rhetorical openers.
- Summary closings: "In conclusion...", "Overall...".
- Transition words: however, additionally, furthermore, moreover.
- **Em dashes (—). Zero. Ever.** Use commas or "..." instead. (Also a hard Slumbor rule.)

## Diagnostic before shipping copy
Read it aloud as if texting a friend. Count: <60% SVO, <40% of sentences start with
"I"/"The", zero transition words, zero banned vocab. For casual copy also: ≥25% of
sentences ≤5 words, ≥10% ≥30 words, ≥1 filler per 50 words, ≥1 lived-experience
detail per 100 words.

## Meta-rule
If it feels like it's "flowing well / sounding professional," it's probably AI. Real
casual writing feels slightly uncomfortable to write. For page copy, keep the grammar
clean but still kill the polish-instinct tells.
